The hot tub (jacuzzi) is **not a children's playground** — it's a space for relaxation and therapy, intended primarily for adults. To keep things hygienic, safe, and pleasant for all guests, please **strictly follow** the rules below.

## ⚠️ Rules of use

- Use the hot tub **at your own risk**.
- **Always shower before** entering.
- **No entry** with dirty feet or with anything brought in from the yard or nature (soil, grass, sand…) — and **no pets**.
- Children may only use the jacuzzi **under constant adult supervision**.
- No jumping, diving, or any rough behaviour.
- Recommended duration: **maximum 2 × 20 minutes**.
- People with **open wounds, skin infections, or medical conditions must not use the tub**.
- **No food, drinks, or glass items** in the jacuzzi.

## 💧 Water care

If the water becomes cloudy or anything seems off, please contact your host immediately.

If you're staying more than 2–3 days and you're familiar with how it works, you can add a multifunctional tablet for water care — it's in the wooden chest next to the jacuzzi.

## 🛡️ After use

**Replace the cover** so flying creatures and other small animals don't accidentally end up in the water, and so the temperature is maintained for next time.

---

Not following the hygiene and safety rules can increase the risk of skin irritation and infection. Thank you for your understanding and responsible use. 💚
