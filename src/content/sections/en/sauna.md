Light the fire **3 hours before** you want to use the sauna — that's how long the stones need to reach **~60 °C**.

## 🪵 Lighting the fire

1. **Open the chimney damper** — without this, smoke goes into the room.
2. Lay **paper + small kindling**, then **dry hardwood** (beech, oak). Not too much at once — leave room for air.
3. Close the firebox door. Control airflow with the **ash drawer**: more open = stronger fire.
4. Add wood as needed — less, more often.

**Don't burn:** treated or painted wood, plastic, chemical fire starters, resinous softwood (pine, spruce).

## 🔥 Steam and aromas

Pour water **only on the stones** (never the firebox, never the metal). Use a small ladle.

Add a few drops of **essential oils** to the small stone diffuser (*saunakko*) sitting between the stones, with a little water in it. 🌿

## ⏱️ Duration

In a bio sauna we recommend **up to 30 minutes** per session.

## ⚠️ Safety

- **Never leave the fire unattended.**
- The space **in front of the stove must stay clear** — embers can spit.
- **Nothing on or near the stove** (towel, clothing, wood).
- Drink water before and after; **never after alcohol**.
- Pregnant women, children, and anyone with cardiovascular conditions — consult a doctor first.
- Hot stones and metal — burn risk. Take care.

## 🌒 After use

Let the fire burn out by itself (don't put it out with water). While embers are live, **don't fully close the air supply** — carbon monoxide risk. **Empty the ash only when cold**, ideally the next day.
